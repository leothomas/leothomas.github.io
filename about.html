Professional

Recently, I worked as a backend engineer at a startup called Nar Technologies, in Beirut, Lebanon. Nar was developing an Android app that helps drone operators optimize their infrastructure inspections by managing images and generating reports on the fly.

I refactored the application's AWS backend by migrating to a NoSQL database and GraphQL API and redeployed all the resources with autoscalling. I also implemented versioned Infrastructure-as-Code deployments by generating Cloudformation templates using Troposphere (Python).

Before working with Nar, I was a software engineer and data scientist at a startup called 3D4Pro, in Paris, France. 3D4Pro offered 3D modelling services and consulting expertise in distributed manufacturing, including 3D printing.

I helped develop a web-app to allow customers and CAD experts to collaborate on 3D modelling projects; I developed webscrapers to (politely) gather data from 3D freelancing websites and analysed it in order to better understand the nature of the customers' projects and identify pain-points in the modelling process. I also performed statistical analysis on the firm's past projects, in order to better predict the time needed to generate a 3D model.

Quantum Computing Research

In my last year at McGill, I did a 6 month research project on quantum computation. The idea was to simulate the way entangled quantum particles transfer information when random disorder is present in the entanglement channel. The simulation was written in Python, using Scipy and a lot of matrix operations.
